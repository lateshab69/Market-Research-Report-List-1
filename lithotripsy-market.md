<p><p>The lithotripsy market comprises three main types: extracorporeal lithotripsy (ESWL), which uses shock waves generated outside the body to break kidney stones; intracorporeal lithotripsy, where instruments are introduced directly into the body to fragment stones using laser, ultrasound, or mechanical methods; and percutaneous lithotripsy, a minimally invasive procedure that involves accessing the kidney through the skin to remove stones. Each type offers distinct advantages, catering to various patient needs and stone characteristics in urological treatments.</p></p>

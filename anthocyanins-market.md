<p><p>The anthocyanins market is categorized primarily into four types: cyanidin, malvidin, delphinidin, and peonidin. Cyanidin, known for its red hue, is commonly found in cherries and strawberries, while malvidin, prevalent in red wine, imparts a deep purple color. Delphinidin, often associated with blue flowers, offers unique coloring properties, and peonidin, found in plants like hibiscus, provides a pink to purple shade. These variants are sought after in food, beverages, cosmetics, and nutraceuticals for their natural coloring and health benefits.</p></p>

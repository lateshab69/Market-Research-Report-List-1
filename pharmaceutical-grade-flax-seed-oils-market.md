<p><p>The pharmaceutical-grade flax seed oils market is characterized by two primary extraction methods: the squeezing method and the leaching method. The squeezing method involves mechanically pressing the seeds, preserving their nutritional quality and active compounds, making it preferred for health applications. In contrast, the leaching method utilizes solvents to extract oil, which can enhance yield but may compromise purity. Both methods cater to different market segments, emphasizing the importance of extraction technique in determining oil quality and intended use.</p></p>

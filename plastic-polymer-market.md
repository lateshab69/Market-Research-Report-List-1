<p><ul><li>Thermoplastic Polymers</li><li>Thermosetting Polymers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The plastic polymer market is broadly categorized into **thermoplastic polymers** and **thermosetting polymers**. Thermoplastics are materials that soften when heated and harden upon cooling, allowing them to be reshaped and recycled multiple times. Examples include polyethylene and polypropylene. Thermosetting polymers, on the other hand, undergo irreversible chemical changes when heated, forming rigid, durable structures that cannot be remolded. Examples include epoxy and phenolic resins. Both types serve diverse industries, with thermoplastics favored for recyclability and thermosets for high-performance applications.</p></p>

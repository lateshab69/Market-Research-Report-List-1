<p><p>The dehydrators for vegetables and fruits market comprises three primary types: conduction, convection, and radiation. </p><p>Conduction type dehydrators utilize direct heat transfer through solid materials, effectively removing moisture but often unevenly. Convection type employs airflow to circulate heated air around the produce, ensuring uniform drying. Radiation type uses infrared or other radiant heat sources to dehydrate foods, often reducing drying times significantly. Each type has distinct advantages suited to varying needs, influencing consumer choices and market trends in food preservation.</p></p>

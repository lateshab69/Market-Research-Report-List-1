<p><strong>The Resistant Starch Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>RS1</li><li>RS2</li><li>RS3</li><li>RS4</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The resistant starch market comprises four main types: RS1, RS2, RS3, and RS4. RS1 is found in whole grains and seeds, offering a physical barrier to digestion. RS2 is present in raw high-amylose foods like unripe bananas, while RS3 forms during cooking and cooling starchy foods, such as rice and potatoes. RS4 is chemically modified starch, designed for specific applications in food processing. Each type offers unique health benefits, attracting attention for their roles in digestive health and blood sugar management.</p></p>

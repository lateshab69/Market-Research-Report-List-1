<p><p>The sodium-ion battery market features two primary types of anode materials: hard carbon and soft carbon. Hard carbon, typically produced through carbonization of organic precursors, offers high cycling stability and good sodium storage capabilities but has lower capacity compared to soft carbon. Conversely, soft carbon provides higher capacity and better rate performance, but may compromise cycling stability. The choice between these materials is critical for optimizing battery performance, influencing factors like energy density, lifespan, and overall efficiency in various applications.</p></p>

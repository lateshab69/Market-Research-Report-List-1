<p><p>Surveying-grade LiDAR encompasses three main market types: Near-Infrared LiDAR, Visible LiDAR, and Ultraviolet LiDAR. Near-Infrared LiDAR is primarily used for vegetation mapping and topographical surveys, leveraging light that can penetrate foliage. Visible LiDAR captures detailed surface features and colors, making it suitable for urban planning and infrastructure projects. Ultraviolet LiDAR, while less common, is effective for detecting materials and assessing environmental monitoring, using shorter wavelengths for precision in specific applications. Together, they serve diverse surveying needs across various industries.</p></p>

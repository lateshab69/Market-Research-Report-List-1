<p><p>The thin-film semiconductor deposition market encompasses various techniques for creating thin films used in electronics. Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) involves the chemical reaction of gaseous precursors to form solid films on substrates, suitable for high-quality films in integrated circuits and solar cells. Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD), on the other hand, utilizes physical processes like evaporation or sputtering to deposit thin films, commonly used in coatings and microelectronic devices. Both methods are essential for advanced semiconductor manufacturing technologies.</p></p>
